Timothy's Café's Recipes

I adapted this recipe from a book called Sydney Food by Bill Grainger. Ever since tasting these eggs on my 1st visit to Bill's restaurant in King's Cross, these eggs have been the best eggs I have ever tasted

This recipe is what I call a "very speical breakfast"; just look at the ingredients to see you. It has to be tasted to belive

Ingredients

The following ingredients make one serving:

  • 2 eggs
  • 1 tablespoon of butter (10g)
  • 1/3 cup of cream (Two 3/4 fl ounces)
  • A pinch of salt
  • Freshly milled black pepper
  • 3 fresh chives (chopped)
  1. Whisk eggs, cream, and salt in a bowl.
  2. Melt the butter in a non-stick pan over a high heat (taking care not to burn the butter).
  3. Pour egg mixture into a pan and wait until it starts setting around the edge of the pan (around 20 seconds).
  4. Using a wooden spatula, bring the mixture into the center as if it were an omlet, and let it cook for another 20 secinds
  5. Fold contents in again, leave for 20 seconds, and repeat until eggs are only just done.
  6. Grind a light sprinkling of freshly milled pepper over the eggs and blend in some chopped fresh chives

You should only make a maximum of two servings per frying pan
